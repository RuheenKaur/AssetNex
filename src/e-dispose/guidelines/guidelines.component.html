E-Waste Disposal Eligibility Checklist

An IT asset can be considered for e-disposal only if it meets the following conditions:

  • End of Warranty Period: The assetâ€™s warranty has expired. No extended coverage is active.
  • Outdated or Unsupported Technology: Hardware is incompatible with current software or security standards. Cannot be upgraded for use.
  • Physical Damage: Non-functional due to hardware failure, damage, or wear. Repair is not possible or cost-effective.
  • No Assigned User / Unused for > 6 Months: Asset is unassigned or inactive for over 6 months.
  • Asset Already Marked as Inactive or Retired: Removed from active inventory and flagged for decommissioning.
  • Data Wiped or Decommissioned: Sensitive data has been wiped per IT policy. Certified data erasure will be completed before disposal.
  • Approval from IT/Admin Department: Disposal request must be evaluated and approved by IT/Admin.
  • Not Under Legal or Audit Hold: The asset is not involved in any audit, compliance check, or legal inquiry.
